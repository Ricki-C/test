What would those extra hot days change?

A hotter year isn't just a redder map. It shows up in your sleep, your child's report card, your power bill, and your local ER.

Scenario
California · 2050 · SSP3-7.0
47 days vs ~12 today
Sleep
−11hrs / yr
from −3 hrs today

Warm nights shorten sleep. By 2099 the gap could double — the West Coast hit roughly twice as hard as inland regions.

Minor et al. (2022), One Earth
Learning
−5%school yr
from −1% today

Days above 90°F lower PSAT scores. Without AC the loss is ~30% larger, and 3× larger for Black and Hispanic students.

Park, Goodman, Hurwitz & Smith (2020), AEJ
Cooling cost
+$200/ summer
from current bills

AC alone can add $72–$108 a month per household — and 1 in 5 low-income U.S. households have no AC at all.

U.S. EIA (2024)
ER visits
+35k/ summer
from observed baseline

Hot days drive cardiovascular, respiratory, and mental-health ER visits — costing ~$1B per summer nationally.

Stanford / UCSD (2025); Nori-Sarma (2022)
But not everyone feels it equally

Low-income students lose 3× more learning. Outdoor workers lose 14% of their labor capacity above 90°F. 1 in 5 households can't afford to turn on AC. Climate change doesn't hit a country — it hits the people least equipped to cool down.

Park et al. (2020); HEAT-SHIELD (2020); EIA (2024)
